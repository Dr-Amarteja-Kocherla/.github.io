About

I build embedded sensing and non-destructive evaluation (NDE) systems that turn passive concrete into intelligent, data-reporting infrastructure. My work bridges materials science, signal processing, and structural diagnostics to enable Construction 4.0 — real-time quality control for automated construction, life-cycle structural health monitoring (SHM), and data-driven certification of reclaimed components.

I am currently a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Fellow at BAM Berlin, where I lead the InSenS-3DCP project on inline sensing for 3D concrete printing. Earlier, I was a postdoctoral associate at NYU Abu Dhabi (2023–2025) and an Assistant Professor at VNRVJIET, Hyderabad (2021–2022). I completed my PhD at IIT Hyderabad in 2021 under Prof. K.V.L. Subramaniam.

Research interests

Embedded PZT sensing Electromechanical impedance (EMI) Coda wave interferometry Acoustic emission 3D concrete printing Structural health monitoring Low-carbon binders Digital twins & IoT Circular construction

I work along the full chain from custom transducer design to field deployment: PZT sensor packaging, multi-channel data acquisition, physics-informed signal processing, finite-element modelling of sensor–material coupling, and lab-to-site validation campaigns. Recent work focuses on inline impedance-based monitoring of extrusion-based 3D concrete printing and on field SHM of bridges using embedded and surface-mounted PZT arrays.

Selected projects

InSenS-3DCP — Inline Sensing for 3D Concrete Printing

2025 – 2027 · Marie Skłodowska-Curie Postdoctoral Fellowship · Grant No. 101151488 · €189,687 (PI)

Develops impedance-based sensor arrays embedded in extruded filaments to provide real-time feedback on rheology, setting, and internal defects during 3D concrete printing. Output: closed-loop print-control prototype and a draft RILEM QPA protocol for sensor-based 3DCP quality control.

Intrinsic Self-Sensing Concrete (AMBER Lab, NYU Abu Dhabi)

2023 – 2025

Commissioned the Intrinsic Self-Sensing Research Unit at NYUAD; supervised junior researchers on EMI-based monitoring of high-performance cementitious systems; delivered consulting on rock testing and CFRP laminate strengthening.

Embedded PZT Sensor Arrays for Damage Detection (PhD, IIT Hyderabad)

2015 – 2021 · Advisor: Prof. K.V.L. Subramaniam

Developed the electromechanical impedance framework for embedded PZT sensors used for hydration and damage monitoring in concrete. Results protected by Indian Patent No. 554635 (granted 2023).
